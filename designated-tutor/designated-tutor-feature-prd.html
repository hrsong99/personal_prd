1. Goal

Let students browse, favorite, and book classes with specific PODO tutors — parallel to (not replacing) the current "any available tutor" flow.

2. Scope summary

Five user-asked surfaces + one foundational surface (favorites, the inverse of the existing le_tutor_exclusion).

Step 3+ → §4.1 Booking screen

After tapping 예약하기 on a lesson card, the user lands on the §4.1 booking screen with classId set. From there:

  • Pick a time slot — pink slots indicate favorite-tutor availability.
  • Tap → confirm dialog. If pink + user has tickets → "튜터 선택하기" row appears.
  • Tap "튜터 선택하기" → §4.2 slot-scoped tutor picker.
  • Confirm → booked.

How tutor-discovery paths re-enter the primary flow

There is no standalone tutor list page. The only "tutor list" in the app is the slot-scoped picker (§4.2), reached mid-booking. Tutor profiles (§4.3) are reachable only from there, or from /my-podo/tutor-favorites (§4.7).

Entry pointWhat it does
§4.3 profile "이 튜터로 예약하기" CTA (from my-podo favorites)Auto-picks the user's next-up lesson in their active curriculum and routes to §4.1 with both classId + tutor preference set. The booking screen's confirm dialog opens with "튜터 선택하기 → Jenny" pre-filled.
No active curriculum (new user, between courses)Same CTA opens the lesson tab with a sticky banner: "Jenny와 예약 중 · 레슨을 먼저 골라주세요." Tutor preference persists in session state until a lesson is picked.
§4.2 picker card bodyTap to book immediately — opens confirm dialog with tutor + slot + ticket pre-filled.
§4.2 picker "프로필 →" linkOpens tutor profile in preview mode. Back-button returns to the picker with state preserved.
♥ heart on any cardFavorites the tutor. No navigation. The favorite is reflected by pink chips on any §4.1 booking screen where the tutor has open slots.
Why one canonical flow instead of two
  • Curriculum progress is the anchor in PODO. A user is always "at lesson N of Level X" — that's the natural mental model for what to book next.
  • Two parallel flows would diverge on edge cases — what if the user starts from the tutor profile but is between curricula? What if their active curriculum has no upcoming lessons? Forcing a single flow makes these handle-once-not-twice.
  • Tutor preference is state, not a separate flow. Carrying "user prefers Jenny" through the funnel as a sticky banner + auto-filled confirm dialog row gives the user the discovery-first feeling without splitting the funnel.
  • "이 튜터로 예약하기" defaults to next-up lesson — handles 80% of cases. If a user wants a non-next lesson with this tutor, they can pull the curriculum sticky open and pick manually.

Card design

  • 72px square portrait with audio 🔊 overlaid bottom-left.
  • 17px bold name as the visual title; flag + NEW/POPULAR badges inline.
  • Heart anchored to card's top-right (favorite/unfavorite without leaving picker).
  • 한국어 가능 inline on rating row when applicable.
  • 함께한 N회 in green; muted "함께한 적 없음" when 0.
  • "신규" instead of "0.0" until review count ≥ 5.
  • 휴식 중 tutors hidden server-side.
Tap behavior — card body vs. profile button
  • Card body tap → opens booking confirm dialog with this tutor + slot + ticket pre-filled. One tap from picker to confirmation.
  • "프로필 →" tap → opens §4.3 tutor profile in preview mode. Back-button returns to picker with state preserved.
  • ♥ heart tap → favorites/unfavorites without leaving the picker. The card may move between 찜한 튜터 / 다른 튜터 sections on next refresh.
What we deliberately removed (vs. a standalone tutor list)
  • No language tab. Curriculum already pins the language. A tab here would create a false affordance.
  • No sort/filter sheets. Slot context narrows the list to ~15 tutors max; one inline sort chip ("⌄ 평점 높은 순") is enough.
  • No search bar. Same reason — short list.
  • No "전체 보기 →" to a destination favorites page. My-podo favorites (§4.7.1) is the only place for cross-slot favorite management.
Edge cases
  • 0 favorites available at this slot → 찜한 튜터 section hidden; only 다른 튜터 shown.
  • Already-favorited resting tutor → still in /my-podo/tutor-favorites with 휴식 중 pill; removed from picker availability counts.

Per-slot endpoint

GET /api/v3/podo/schedule/{classId}/favorite-counts?date=…
→ { "09:00": 3, "09:30": 1, … }

4.6 — 지정튜터 티켓 purchase /tutors/purchase-ticket

Pricing constraints
  • Tutor cost: EN ≈ ₩2,000/lesson, JP ≈ ₩3,500/lesson.
  • Floor: minimum 10% premium on tutor cost, even at deepest bulk tier.
  • Premium signal: 1-ticket anchored above subscription per-lesson cost.
  • Bulk incentive: meaningful discount at 5/10/20 회권, never below the 10% floor.

Recommended pricing (English vs Japanese)

🇺🇸 English tutor cost: ₩2,000/회
회권 회당 합계 1회권 대비 원가 대비
1회 ₩4,000 ₩4,000 — +100%
5회 ₩3,400 ₩17,000 −15% +70%
10회 ⭐ ₩2,900 ₩29,000 −28% +45%
20회 ₩2,400 ₩48,000 −40% +20%
🇯🇵 Japanese tutor cost: ₩3,500/회
회권 회당 합계 1회권 대비 원가 대비
1회 ₩7,000 ₩7,000 — +100%
5회 ₩5,950 ₩29,750 −15% +70%
10회 ⭐ ₩5,000 ₩50,000 −29% +43%
20회 ₩4,200 ₩84,000 −40% +20%
Margin logic + "가장 인기" anchor choice
  • 1회권 = 100% markup over tutor cost. Anchors single-lesson value at 2× so every bulk tier feels like a real discount.
  • Bulk steps: +100% → +70% → +45% → +20%. Shopper sees −15% / −28% / −40% off 1회권.
  • 20회권 at +20% — twice the 10% floor, leaves room for promo stacking.
  • JP = 1.75× EN at every tier (matches tutor-cost ratio). Same discount schedule across languages.
  • SKU floor guardrail: backend rejects SKUs where price/total_count < tutor_cost × 1.10.
  • 10회권 is the "가장 인기" anchor — −28% feels substantial; wallet hit (₩29k EN / ₩50k JP) stays under 1-month subscription, so it doesn't cannibalize.

8. Rollout & seeding policies

Two separate levers: (a) who gets access, and (b) how they get their first ticket. Both are worth piloting; the design lets us do them sequentially.

8.1 — Heavy-user gate (recommended for v1)

Limit the feature flag to students with 10~20+ cumulative completed lessons. They've met enough tutors to have real preferences, their LTV is the most at risk if they churn from boredom, and they've already proven willingness to pay. Cohort size from completed_lesson_count ≥ 10 is the target for early metrics.

Why this cohort & promotion criteria
  • Established preferences: heavy users have tutors they like (and dislike). Favoriting is meaningful for them on day one.
  • Highest churn risk if bored: heavy users plateau hardest. A new mechanic gives them a reason to come back.
  • Willingness to pay is already proven: they renewed at least once.
  • Strong feedback signal: small cohort, deeply invested → better qualitative signal than mass rollout.
  • Promote to GA when: ticket purchase rate ≥ target % of heavy users who try it · avg lessons per buyer rises · no margin/refund anomalies.

8.2 — Free seeding tickets (bootstrap demand)

Grant 2~3 free designated-tutor tickets on first /tutors entry to remove the "try before buy" friction. Tickets marked source='SEED' in the table so we can analyze seed-vs-paid behavior separately. 30-day expiry (shorter than purchased 90d) to create urgency.

What we learn & risk acceptance
  • Conversion rate: of heavy users given 3 free tickets, what % buy more?
  • Repeat behavior: do they re-book the same tutor or experiment with several?
  • Time to first repurchase: a fast repurchase signals product-market fit.
  • Risk accepted: heavy users burn freebies and never buy. That's exactly the negative result we need before scaling spend.
  • Cost ceiling: 3 free × N heavy users × tutor cost. With N ~ a few thousand heavy users at EN ₩2k tutor cost, the bill is bounded (~few million KRW).

8.3 — Compensation tickets for unlimited subscribers

Give unlimited-plan users 1 designated ticket for every 3~4 consecutive days they don't take a class. Cheap retention lever: unlimited users feel guilty about skipping days; this turns the skip into a small reward instead of a regret.

Mechanics, variants & gaming risk
  • Trigger: a cron checks each unlimited user's last lesson date; on the 3rd or 4th day of inactivity, grants 1 ticket.
  • Stockpile cap: max 4 compensation tickets accumulated — can't farm forever.
  • Expiry: 30 days from grant (urgency to come back).
  • SKU tag: source='COMP_UNLIMITED' for analytics.
  • Why 3 vs 4 days: 3-day version is more generous (higher engagement, higher cost); 4-day is conservative. Recommend starting with 4 days, watching ticket-burn rate, and tightening or loosening from there.
  • Cost framing: unlimited users were paying us anyway. Compensation only costs tutor payout on the redeemed designated lessons — and a redeemed lesson means an engaged user, which is the entire goal.
  • Gaming risk: users could intentionally skip to farm tickets. Acceptable because (a) cap prevents long-term abuse, (b) the skipping behavior IS what we want to convert.

Technical phasing (existing infra view)

Phase 1 — discovery only: /tutors, profile (no booking CTA), my-podo favorites row, tutor-led lesson cards. Visible to heavy-user cohort.

Phase 2 — commerce + booking: ticket purchase, pink-chip booking, designated booking, seed grant on first /tutors entry.

Phase 3 — compensation tickets: daily cron, grant logic, abuse cap.

Phase 4 (v1.1): public review free-text, push on favorite-opens-slot, tutor recommendations.
